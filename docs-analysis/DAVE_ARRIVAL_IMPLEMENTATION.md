## Key Addition: First Aunt Mae Visit Together

The most significant new content is the ending of Chapter 4, which bridges Dave's arrival with the shared refuge of Chapter 5:

```
That afternoon, we made ice cream together - all three of us. Aunt Mae brought
out the wooden bucket and metal canister with ceremony. Dave watched, fascinated,
as we cracked eggs and measured sugar and poured vanilla. When it came time to
crank, I grasped the wooden handle.

"Want to help?" I asked Dave.

He nodded eagerly, his small hands joining mine on the crank. At first it was
easy, the handle spinning freely as we laughed and took turns. But as the ice
cream formed, the resistance grew. My shoulders started to burn. Dave's arms
trembled with effort.

That's when Aunt Mae's hands covered both of ours.

"We're almost there, Shugs," she said softly, her strength supplementing ours
without taking over. Together - all six hands on that wooden handle - we pushed
through the final hard minutes until finally, the crank wouldn't turn anymore.

"There we go," Aunt Mae said, triumph and gentleness in equal measure. "We did
it together."

Dave's face shone with something I recognized: the feeling of being invited in,
of contributing something that mattered, of hands covering yours when the work
got too hard.

Dave had found refuge too.
```

**Why this works:**
- Mirrors the solo ice cream scene from Ch 3
- Shows Dave experiencing unconditional love immediately
- "All six hands" - beautiful image of shared refuge
- "We did it together" takes on new meaning
- Sets up Chapter 5's shared refuge naturally
