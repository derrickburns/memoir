#### B. Chapter Quality Checklist

**Added new section after Voice Preservation Checklist:**

**For EVERY chapter, always check for:**

1. **Weak Character Development**
   - Every character must advance a through-line OR be cut
   - Ask: "What does this character reveal about Derrick's path to finding himself and thriving?"

2. **Boring Detail Not Relevant to Narrative Arc**
   - Details must serve emotional truth or through-line advancement
   - "Interesting" ‚â† "necessary"
   - Ask: "Does this detail help the reader understand how Derrick finds himself and learns to thrive, or is it just atmospheric?"

3. **Too Much Telling, Not Enough Showing**
   - Show through concrete scenes with dialogue and action
   - Cut adult reflective analysis that explains what scenes already showed
   - Ask: "Am I showing the reader what happened, or explaining to them what it meant?"

4. **Concepts Introduced Without Proper Context**
   - Introduce concepts with enough context for reader to understand
   - Don't assume reader knows references
   - Ground abstract concepts in concrete examples FIRST
   - Ask: "Would a reader who knows nothing about this understand what I'm referring to?"
