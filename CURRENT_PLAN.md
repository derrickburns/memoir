## Key New Content Added

### 1. First Aunt Mae Visit Together (Chapter 4 ending)
Shows Dave experiencing unconditional love for the first time:
> "That afternoon, we made ice cream together - all three of us... When it came time to crank, I grasped the wooden handle. 'Want to help?' I asked Dave. He nodded eagerly, his small hands joining mine... That's when Aunt Mae's hands covered both of ours. 'We're almost there, Shugs,' she said softly... Together - all six hands on that wooden handle - we pushed through... 'We did it together.' Dave had found refuge too."

### 2. Refuge Lost Section (Chapter 6)
Explains when and how Aunt Mae visits stopped at age 10:
> "Those weekend visits to Aunt Mae's stopped by the time I turned ten. I don't remember the conversation, if there even was one... The loss settled over Dave and me like a fog... Without Aunt Mae's house as counterbalance, home felt heavier. The conditional love more obvious. The need to perform more urgent."
